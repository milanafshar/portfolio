Bad Bunny’s halftime show became the conversation

This story is about one halftime show only. I’m using clips and live reactions to show how a performance turns into news the moment it hits TV and social media.

What happened

Bad Bunny headlined the Apple Music Super Bowl Halftime Show and leaned into Spanish-language hits, big visuals and a clear cultural point of view. The performance quickly moved beyond the stadium, it became a debate online about music, identity, and who halftime shows are “for.”

My reflection

Putting the clips and reactions on one page helped me understand how interactive news works: the reader can move between the original performance and the public response without leaving the story. Embedding tools was easier than I expected, but I also learned that some embeds can be unreliable, so I included clear source links and fallback text.
